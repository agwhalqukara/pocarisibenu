Is Courtney Zieller Married? Her Wiki, Age, Family, Height &amp; Facts

The storytelling skills of Courtney Zieller is one of the many reasons to be loved by the viewers. She has covered stories from inside state penitentiaries and has interviewed numerous political and prominent figures.

Courtney Zieller is an American journalist, currently serving as a news reporter at WFSB, a CBS-affiliated station in Hartford, Connecticut, the United States. She is now working with the Eyewitness News 3 team.

Courtney Zieller Wiki; Age, Family

Born as Courtney Anne Zieller in the year 1980, she is 40 years old by age. Her birthday falls on the 16th of December that makes her birth sign Sagittarius.

Zieller was raised by her parents Richard Zieller and Elaine Zieller. She grew up along with a sister named Brittney Zieller in Wethersfield, Connecticut, the United States.

Education, Height

Courtney attended Wethersfield High School in her hometown, graduating in 1999. After high school, she went to Central Connecticut State University and graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in Mass Media Communication.

Moreover, she took advanced journalism classes at New York University from 2004 to 2005.

She is yet to reveal her actual height to the media. But in one of her tweets, she stated that she is almost equal to the height of American basketball player Shabazz Napier. Meanwhile, Shabazz stands 6 feet (1.83 meters) tall.

Her Career; WFSB, KSFY, KELO

Courtney interned at WWLP-TV, a dual NBC/CW+-affiliated television station in Springfield, Massachusetts. She began her journalism career in 2006 from the CBS-affiliate WHBF-TV in Rock Island, Illinois.

After working for two years, she left the station and joined a dual ABC/MyNetworkTV-affiliated WQAD-TV. After the brief stint, she moved to KELO-TV, a dual CBS/MyNetworkTV-affiliated television station in Sioux Falls, South Dakota in 2008.

There, she produced and reported the news during her two-year stint. Similarly, from 2010 to 2012, she worked as an anchor and reporter for KSFY-TV.

In 2012, she joined WFSB, a CBS-affiliated station in Hartford, Connecticut. She now serves as a news reporter for the Eyewitness News 3 team for the network.
