Patterson Hood is one of the most popular and richest Singer-Songwriter who was born on March 24, 1964 in Muscle Shoals, Alabama, United States.

Hood has been married two times. He has two children, Ava Ruth Hood and Emmett Hood, and has lived in Athens, Georgia since April 1, 1994. Hood now lives with his wife in Portland, Oregon, he said on July 27, 2018 at a show in Omaha, Nebraska. In July 2015, Hood was featured in a New York Times editorial titled “The South’s Heritage Is So Much More Than a Flag” which discusses the misrepresentation of the history of the Confederate flag in the Southern United States.

After Adam’s House Cat split up, Hood and Cooley continued to work together. They eventually formed the Drive-By Truckers in 1996, following a mutual relocation to Athens, GA.
